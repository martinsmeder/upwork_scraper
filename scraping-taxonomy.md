# Scraping Taxonomy

This taxonomy was used for creating scraping-demand.txt (based on scraping-jobs-summary.json).

### `delivery_type`

These labels describe the primary form of work the client is buying.

- `One-time dataset`: one-off scrape, research pull, or exported file delivered once.
- `Recurring data feed`: scheduled or ongoing delivery of refreshed data.
- `Custom scraping system`: build a scraper, bot, actor, script, extension, framework, or multi-source extraction system for a defined use case, whether simple or intended to scale.
- `Workflow automation`: scraping is one part of a broader automation that includes integrations, alerts, AI processing, publishing, or downstream actions.
- `Maintenance / optimization`: improve, debug, scale, or maintain an existing scraping or browser automation system.
- `Advisory / content / go-to-market`: non-build support such as legal advice, SEO, technical writing, community, or business development.

### `solution_category`

These labels describe the main client outcome or business problem being solved.

- `Lead generation and contact discovery`: prospect lists, emails, phone numbers, contact forms, or outreach-ready lead data.
- `Business, provider, and location directory intelligence`: company, provider, school, store, dispensary, POI, or organization profile coverage for search, marketplaces, listings, or internal entity databases.
- `Product, inventory, and pricing intelligence`: product catalogs, inventory, specifications, hotel rates, pricing, or marketplace item data.
- `Real estate, rental, and property intelligence`: property listings, rentals, permits, construction, or real-estate market data.
- `Social media and creator intelligence`: posts, comments, DMs, profiles, influencers, trends, chats, or other social/community data.
- `Public records, legal, and compliance data`: government records, legal/compliance datasets, tender portals, or regulated/public-sector information.
- `Events, jobs, and opportunity monitoring`: events, conference attendees, RFPs, jobs, notices, or other time-sensitive opportunity data.
- `Document library extraction and content migration`: extracting files, pages, forms, or media from a source system for reuse, archiving, migration, or structured storage.
- `Market research and competitive intelligence`: competitor lists, market signals, rankings, ad presence, review capture, or brand/industry research.
- `Generic scraping engineering`: generic scraper build, extraction, scaling, monitoring, maintenance, or crawler-platform work where the post does not point to a clearer business-domain outcome.
- `Not specified`: use only when the post is too vague to classify confidently.
